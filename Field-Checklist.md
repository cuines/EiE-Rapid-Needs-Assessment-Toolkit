# EiE Rapid Needs Assessment Field Checklist

Use this checklist during field visits to ensure key data points are captured systematically. Tick (✓) each item as it is completed.

## Pre‑Field Preparation
- [ ] Coordinate with local authorities and security focal points.
- [ ] Obtain necessary permissions for site visits and interviews.
- [ ] Prepare assessment tools (questionnaires, observation forms, maps).
- [ ] Assemble assessment team and assign roles.
- [ ] Brief team on safety protocols and ethical guidelines.

## Site Arrival and Introduction
- [ ] Introduce team and purpose of assessment to community leaders.
- [ ] Obtain informed consent from participants (verbal or written).
- [ ] Confirm language preferences and arrange for interpreters if needed.

## Access and Enrollment Data
- [ ] Record total number of school‑aged children in the community (disaggregated by sex and age).
- [ ] Document number of out‑of‑school children (disaggregated by sex, age, disability).
- [ ] Identify main barriers to enrollment (safety, distance, cost, etc.).
- [ ] Collect pre‑crisis enrollment figures from school records or authorities.

## Learning Spaces and Infrastructure
- [ ] Count functional schools/learning spaces in the area.
- [ ] Assess physical condition of buildings (damage, safety hazards).
- [ ] Verify availability of water, sanitation, and hygiene (WASH) facilities.
- [ ] Check safe access routes to learning spaces (roads, bridges, security).
- [ ] Note any temporary learning spaces (tents, community buildings).

## Teaching Capacity
- [ ] Record number of available teachers (disaggregated by sex, qualifications).
- [ ] Discuss teacher absenteeism and turnover with school management.
- [ ] Identify immediate training needs (psychosocial support, inclusive pedagogy).
- [ ] Inventory available teaching and learning materials (textbooks, chalk, etc.).

## Curriculum and Certification
- [ ] Determine status of formal curriculum (suspended, adapted, etc.).
- [ ] Inquire about alternative education programs (accelerated learning, catch‑up classes).
- [ ] Clarify certification and examination pathways for displaced learners.

## Protection and Well‑being
- [ ] Ask about reported incidents of violence, harassment, or exploitation in/around schools.
- [ ] Map available referral mechanisms for child protection cases.
- [ ] Identify psychosocial support services for learners and teachers.
- [ ] Check for school‑based health and nutrition services.

## Safety and Ethical Compliance
- [ ] Ensure no participant is put at risk by the assessment.
- [ ] Protect anonymity and confidentiality of sensitive information.
- [ ] Use gender‑balanced and culturally sensitive interview techniques.
- [ ] Actively seek perspectives of marginalized groups (girls, children with disabilities, ethnic minorities).

## Post‑Visit Actions
- [ ] Debrief assessment team immediately after the visit.
- [ ] Organize and secure collected data (notes, photos, recordings).
- [ ] Share preliminary findings with community representatives as appropriate.
- [ ] Schedule follow‑up visits if needed.
